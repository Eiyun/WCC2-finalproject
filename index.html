The name HN-001 is derived from the initials Human and Nature, the embodiment of the relationship between man and nature.

Introduction

The device interacts by recognising the participant's gestures and the participant can manipulate the physical device to feel the relationship between humans and nature.

Concept and Background Research

The urban sprawl

Urban sprawl is defined as "the spreading of urban developments (such as houses and shopping centers) on undeveloped land near a city." Urban sprawl has been described as the unrestricted growth in many urban areas of housing, commercial development, and roads over large expanses of land, with little concern for urban planning.

The natural environment is greatly affected by urban sprawl. The expansion of cities has led to the loss of natural habitats and increased pollution levels. When natural spaces are replaced by pavement, roads, buildings or parking lots, it affects the ability of animal and plant species to survive. The fragmentation of natural habitats can lead to a reduction in biodiversity and the loss of valuable species. Urban sprawl also affects water resources, soils, and air quality, transforming them to become polluted due to increased human activities. According to Ho et al. (2007), urbanization contributes to pollution emissions, loss of biodiversity and degradation of environmental quality. The impacts of urban sprawl on the natural environment are wide-ranging. The adoption of a "drive-everywhere" culture has led to increased vehicle emissions which exacerbates air pollution, harming the natural environment (Ewing et al., 2016). The nonpoint source pollution caused by increased impermeable surfaces in urban centers contributes to the loss of natural water quality, leading to a reduction in the availability of aquatic habitat (Hough, 2005). Similarly, the land-use changes required for urbanization result in a loss of green space which negatively impacts outdoor recreation areas, public health, and the mental well-being of citizens (Van den Berg et al., 2007).

Reflections on urban sprawl

With today's trend of urban sprawl, it made me reflect on why cities need sprawl rather than rational development, why sprawl is increasing and whether nature is like a lamb to the slaughter in front of mankind.In the past, when technology was not yet developed, humans lived in harmony with nature, but when technology accelerated and humans had the power forged by technology, everything seemed to change and we were free to fantasise about transforming the natural environment and making it a sacrifice for our personal desires. This is the embodiment of the power relationship between humans and nature. Often when those who wield power want to control the dominated, it only takes the simplest of gestures for the wielder to make the dominated a victim of their own desires, and thus power in the hands of humans is the fundamental cause of urban sprawl.

Technical Implementation

Project idea

In this project I have conceived an interactive device that brings together technology, humans and power - a robotic arm - and plan to install it on a miniature landscape. In order to express my thoughts on the relationship with power, participants will experience the act of using power for "urban sprawl" as a way of feeling the relationship between humans and nature. Amongst other things, they will use the simplest of gestures: up, down, left and right as the main interaction of the piece.

Sketch

Programming Code

At first, I intended to use only ml5.js hand skeleton tracking to get information about the user's hands. I read and researched a lot about ml5.js gesture recognition and connecting to Arduino, and initially I solved most of the gesture recognition problems through programming, but in the process of testing the code after trying to use it in conjunction with servo motors, I found that ml5.js could recognise left heel right gestures well, but not up and down gestures well, even with the use of finger skeleton tracking So I had to try and find another way that would work. Eventually I found a gesture sensor called paj7620. After using this gesture recognition sensor, the gesture issues I was having with ml5.js and some glitches and sensitivity issues were resolved, allowing me to test and carry out the project more effectively, so I decided to use a combination of paj7620 and ml5.js gesture recognition to complete the project.

Physical installation

This physical installation consists of two parts, a miniature scene that simulates a natural landscape and a robotic arm made up of servo motors.

The robot arm

I first drew its structure using software, then used a 3D printer to punch out the parts and finally completed the assembly.

Miniature scene

I used a number of composite materials such as newspaper, lime dust, stones and fake grass.

Reflection and Future Development

In the future, I plan to make this project bigger, include more miniature landscapes as well as robotic arms in it, and continue to experiment with the gesture recognition code using ml5.js, aiming to include more interesting gesture recognition in this project than simply going up and down.

Sketch

Self evaluation

I encountered many difficulties while working on the project, for example, on the code side, writing the code for the gestures in the ml5.js gesture recognition library, which turned out not to be feasible during testing, and on the physical programming side, testing the data of each servo motor to make it move as smoothly as possible. These were all great challenges and allowed me to gain many programming skills in the process. The finished project looks a little more monotonous and less flashy in terms of human computer interaction, but I think that's exactly what I was trying to say: "The powerhouse needs only the simplest of actions to make manipulated dedicated to it”. Making the miniature landscape was also a big challenge, and although this creation was more complete, I still overlooked some details, such as matching the physical installation for hidden wiring. But in any case, I gained a lot from this project, especially the knowledge about the code that combines the gesture recognition function of ml5.js with the physical device, and I believe I will use both technologies more often in the future.
